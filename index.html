MBTA Ridership Analysis

Created by Simeon Lichtenstein, Fatima Sayed Eltayed and Meher Kaur


The MBTA, Boston's public transportation system, is a central part of living in Boston. As the main way many people travel, analyzing the MBTA shows how people flow through the city and what is important to them. Is there a larger number of riders when the Red Sox are playing? Do people stay in during the colder or rainier months, so ridership will be less, or are fewer people walking and biking, so ridership will be more? What line is the most popular, and does that change throughout the year? Which stops are the most popular, showing the hubs of Boston? Looking into how people travel can help us answer these deeper questions about city life to give a better understanding of what life is like in Boston.
